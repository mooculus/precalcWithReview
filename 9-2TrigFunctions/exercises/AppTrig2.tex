\begin{exercise}

Solve the equation:
$$\cot (\theta) = -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}.$$

Give exact answers as in the examples. [\textit{Hint}: Draw the associated triangle.]

\begin{enumerate}
\item What is the reference angle $\theta_R$? $\answer{\frac{\pi}{3}}$

\item What is the period of $\cot\theta$? $\answer{\pi}$

\end{enumerate}

Above we have four angles indicated on the unit circle, each measuring counterclockwise from the $x$-axis.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\theta_R = \answer{\frac{\pi}{3}}$.

\item $\theta_A = \answer{\frac{2\pi}{3}}$.

\item $\theta_B = \answer{\frac{4\pi}{3}}$.

\item $\theta_C = \answer{\frac{5\pi}{3}}$.

\item Which angle is the second solution in the period of $\cot \theta$? 
\begin{multipleChoice}
\choice{$\theta_A$}
\choice{$\theta_B$}
\choice{$\theta_C$}
\choice[correct]{There is no second solution in the period of $\cot \theta$.}
\end{multipleChoice}
\end{enumerate}
