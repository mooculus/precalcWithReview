\begin{exercise}

Suppose that $r=f(t)$ is the radius, in centimeters, of a circle at time $t$ minutes, and $A(r)$ is the area, in square centimeters, of a circle of radius $r$ centimeters.

Which of the following statements best explains the meaning of the composite function $(A(f(t))$?

\begin{multipleChoice}  
\choice{The area of a circle, in square centimeters, of radius $r$ centimeters.}  
\choice[correct]{The area of a circle, in square centimeters, at time $t$ minutes.}  
\choice{The radius of a circle, in centimeters, at time$t$ minutes.}  
\choice{The function $f$ of the minutes and the area.}
\choice{None of these choices.}
\end{multipleChoice}  

Suppose that $r=f(t)=t^3$.  Recall that $A(r)=\pi r^2$.  Find $A(f(t))=\answer{\pi t^6}$.

\end{exercise}
