Suppose $g$ is a function which takes as inputs $t$ seconds, and gives the distance $g(t)$ in meters of how far a car moving in a straight line at a constant speed has traveled from time $t=0$. 

\begin{exercise}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Is the function $g$ one-to-one?
\begin{multipleChoice}
\choice{no}
\choice[correct]{yes}
\end{multipleChoice}

\item Is the function $g$ invertible?
\begin{multipleChoice}
\choice{no}
\choice[correct]{yes}
\end{multipleChoice}

\item Which of the following best describes $g^{-1}(t)$?
\begin{multipleChoice}
\choice{How much fuel it takes for the car to travel $t$ meters}
\choice[correct]{How much time it takes for the car to travel $t$ meters}
\choice{How far the car travels in $t$ seconds}
\choice{How much fuel the car uses in $t$ seconds}
\end{multipleChoice}
\end{enumerate}

\end{exercise}
