As we begin to view trigonometric functions as functions of a real variable, we can identify points and intervals of numbers on the real number line with points and intervals of angles on the unit circle. For example, the interval $\left[\frac{\pi}{6}, \frac{\pi}{3}\right]$ corresponds to angles that lie in Quadrant I, as shown below.
