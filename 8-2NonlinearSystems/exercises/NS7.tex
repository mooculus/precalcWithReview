Solve the given system. Check your answer both algebraically and graphically.

$\left\{ \begin{array}{rcr} x^{2}+ y^{2} & = & 16  \\  y - x & = & 4  \end{array} \right.$

\begin{exercise}
How many solutions does the system have?
\begin{multipleChoice}  
\choice{one solution}  
\choice[correct]{two solutions}  
\choice{three solutions}  
\choice{four solutions}  
\choice{five solutions}  
\choice{no solutions}  
\choice{infinite solutions}  
\end{multipleChoice}  
\begin{exercise}
The solution to this system with negative $x$-coordinate is $\left(\answer{-4},\answer{0}\right)$.
\end{exercise}
\end{exercise}
