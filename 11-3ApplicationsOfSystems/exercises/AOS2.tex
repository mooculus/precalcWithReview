\begin{exercise}
Consider the rectangle below whose lower right corner is on the graph of $y = \frac{x}{3}$ under the line $y = 15$, and whose lower left corner is on the $y$-axis. 
\begin{image}
\begin{tikzpicture}
	\begin{axis}[
            domain=-3:3, ymin=0, ymax=9, xmin=0, xmax=3,
            axis lines =center, xlabel=$x$, ylabel=$y$,
            ticks=none,
            every axis y label/.style={at=(current axis.above origin),anchor=south},
            every axis x label/.style={at=(current axis.right of origin),anchor=west},
            axis on top,
          ]
          \addplot [draw=none, fill=fill2, domain=(-1.5:1.5)] {7} \closedcycle;
          \addplot [draw=none, fill=background, domain=(-1.5:1.5)] {2.25} \closedcycle;

          \addplot [very thick,penColor2,domain=(0:3)] {1.5*x};
	\addplot [very thick,penColor2,domain=(-3:0)] {-1.5*x};
          \addplot [very thick,penColor5,domain=(-3:3)] {7};

          \addplot [very thick, penColor] plot coordinates {(1.5,2.25) (-1.5,2.25)};
          \addplot [very thick, penColor] plot coordinates {(1.5,2.25) (1.5,7)};
          \addplot [very thick, penColor] plot coordinates {(1.5,7) (-1.5,7)};
          \addplot [very thick, penColor] plot coordinates {(-1.5,7) (-1.5,2.25)};
          
          \addplot [color=penColor,fill=penColor,only marks,mark=*] coordinates{(1.5,2.25)};  %% closed hole          
          \addplot [color=penColor,fill=penColor,only marks,mark=*] coordinates{(-1.5,2.25)};  %% closed hole          
          \addplot [color=penColor,fill=penColor,only marks,mark=*] coordinates{(1.5,7)};  %% closed hole          
          \addplot [color=penColor,fill=penColor,only marks,mark=*] coordinates{(-1.5,7)};  %% closed hole   

          %\node at (axis cs:0,4.625) [penColor] {$A(x) =$~area};
          \node at (axis cs:1.5,2.25) [anchor=west,penColor] {$(x,y)$};
          \node at (axis cs:1.5,7.2) [anchor=west,penColor] {$(x,15)$};
        \end{axis}
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{image}

Say the rectangle has an area of 168 units$^2$.

There are two possible values for $x$. The smaller one is $\answer{21}$ and the larger one is $\answer{24}$.
